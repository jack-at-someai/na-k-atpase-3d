Central Place Theory (Christaller, 1933) states that hexagonal tessellation maximizes accessibility, minimizes travel distance, and creates natural hierarchies of specialization. The server core sits at the geographic center. Six pillar labs surround it at equal distance. Shared facilities (fabrication, commons, housing) fill the interstitial hexes. Every point on campus is within 2 hex-steps of the core and 1 hex-step of a pillar lab. The property is designed so Charlotte can observe the entire campus as a living substrate — every building, every room, every researcher is a NODE emitting SIGNALs.
