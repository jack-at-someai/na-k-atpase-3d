History & Heritage
One of the oldest and purest pig breeds in the world — from the English Midlands to American pastures
Pre-1800s
The Tamworth breed develops in and around the town of Tamworth in Staffordshire, England. One of the oldest recognized pig breeds, largely unimproved by Asian genetics — retaining the long snout, lean frame, and foraging instincts of ancient English swine.
1812
Sir Robert Peel imports Barbados "Irish Grazier" hogs to Tamworth, England. Cross-breeding refines the breed's distinctive red-gold coloring and lean conformation, solidifying the Tamworth type.
1882
Thomas Bennett imports the first Tamworths to the United States. The breed is prized for its bacon-type carcass — long sides, lean meat, and excellent flavor. Earns the nickname "The Bacon Pig."
1900s
Tamworth Swine Association established in the United States. Breeders organize to maintain pedigree records and promote the breed. Early registrations focused on Midwestern and Southern farms.
2000s–2020s
Heritage pork movement revives interest in the Tamworth. Farm-to-table restaurants, pasture-raised certifications, and consumer demand for flavor over volume create a growing premium market for Tamworth pork.
2026
Sounder partnership proposed — digital registry, breeder portal, heritage pork marketplace, and analytics platform. From paper records to heritage platform.
Current Operations

The Tamworth Swine Association maintains pedigree records and registrations for the Tamworth breed — the oldest pedigree breed of pig and one of the purest genetic lines in the swine world. With approximately 113 registered breeders across 37 states, TSA is a small but passionate community.

The Tamworth is known as "the bacon pig" — producing the longest, leanest sides of any breed. Red-gold coloring, long body, erect ears, and a narrow frame distinguish it from modern commercial breeds. The breed excels in pasture-based systems with strong foraging instincts and outdoor hardiness.

Operations remain entirely manual. The association runs a basic website (tamworthswine.org), processes registrations by mail, and has no CRM, no digital pedigree tools, and no analytics capability. Breeder communication is handled through a mailing list and occasional newsletters.

Despite its tiny membership, TSA sits at the intersection of several growing markets: heritage pork, pasture-raised production, farm-to-table dining, and sustainable agriculture. The breed's story and flavor profile command premium pricing — $8–15/lb retail vs. $3–5/lb commodity pork.

Tamworth's strong foraging instincts, long snout for rooting, and sunburn-resistant red-gold coat make it the ideal pasture pig. Thrives outdoors year-round where commercial breeds fail. Converts forage to meat efficiently.
