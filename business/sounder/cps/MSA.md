## 1. DEFINITIONS

**1.1 "BarnOS"** means Sounder's proprietary swine industry technology platform, including all registry management infrastructure, breeder relationship management tools, performance analytics engines, pedigree intelligence systems, show management modules, and derived data products.

**1.2 "Platform"** means the deployed instance of BarnOS configured for CPS's operational domain, including all digital registration workflows, breeder portal interfaces, pedigree database systems, herdmark management tools, multi-breed registration engines, show management systems, and analytics dashboards specific to CPS's registered breeds and breeder network.

**1.3 "Breeder Network"** means the community of swine breeders registered with CPS, including all active and historical registrants. As of the Effective Date, the Breeder Network comprises approximately **65,412 breeders** across all CPS breed registries.

**1.4 "Breed Standards"** means the official phenotypic and genetic criteria, classification guidelines, registration rules, and evaluation frameworks maintained by CPS for each of its registered breeds, as established and amended by CPS's Breed Committees and governing bodies.

**1.5 "Registration Authority"** means CPS's institutional role as a recognized registry for its registered breeds, including the authority to issue, approve, deny, revoke, and maintain official pedigree registrations, performance records, and herdmark assignments.

**1.6 "Registered Breeds"** means all purebred swine breeds administered by CPS, including but not limited to Berkshire, Chester White, Duroc, Hampshire, Hereford, Landrace, Poland China, Spotted, Yorkshire, and Tamworth, together with all recognized crossbred designations and any additional breeds CPS may register during the term of this Agreement.

**1.7 "Herdmark"** means a unique alphanumeric identifier assigned by CPS to a registered breeding operation, used to identify the origin herd in all pedigree records and registration documents. As of the Effective Date, CPS maintains approximately **5,279 active herdmarks**.

**1.8 "Herdmark System"** means CPS's institutional framework for issuing, maintaining, transferring, suspending, and retiring herdmarks, including all associated rules, records, and governance procedures.

**1.9 "Show Sanction"** means CPS's official authorization for a swine exhibition or competition to award CPS-recognized placings and champion designations, subject to CPS's show rules and judging standards.

**1.10 "Breed Committee"** means any standing or ad hoc committee established by CPS's governing body to oversee breed standards, registration policies, or genetic improvement programs for a specific registered breed or group of breeds.

**1.11 "Historical Records"** means CPS's existing pedigree databases, registration archives, herdmark registries, performance testing records, show results, breeder directories, and all other institutional data accumulated prior to the Effective Date, including data maintained in CPS's existing online portal and legacy systems.

**1.12 "Existing Portal"** means CPS's current online registration and breeder management system in operation as of the Effective Date, including all associated databases, user accounts, transaction histories, and digital records.

**1.13 "Deliverables"** means the technology platform, digital tools, analytics products, integration services, and operational intelligence outputs produced by Sounder and made available to CPS and the Breeder Network under this Agreement.

**1.14 "Revenue Streams"** means the categories of income generated through the Platform as described in Section 3, including per-registration fees, breeder subscription revenue, data licensing income, show management fees, and herdmark management fees.

**1.15 "Trogdon Farms Founding Partner Agreement"** means the separate agreement between Sounder and Trogdon Farms designating Trogdon Farms as the founding partner of the BarnOS platform, under which Trogdon Farms receives access to BarnOS at no charge in perpetuity. The terms of that agreement are independent of this Agreement.

## 2. SCOPE OF SERVICES AND PARTNERSHIP

**2.1 Partnership Model.** This is a technology partnership, not a standard client engagement. Sounder provides technology infrastructure, platform development, and ongoing technical operations. CPS provides institutional access, registry authority, breed standards governance, herdmark system administration, and breeder network relationships. Both Parties contribute to the success of the Platform and share in the revenue it generates as described in Section 3. The Parties acknowledge the scale and strategic significance of this partnership: CPS's Breeder Network of over 65,000 breeders across all major purebred and crossbred swine designations represents the most comprehensive pedigree swine registry in the United States.

**2.2 Sounder Contributions.** Sounder shall provide:

- (a) **BarnOS Platform** -- Full technology stack including multi-breed digital registration system, breeder portal, pedigree database, herdmark management tools, performance analytics, CRM, show management platform, and crossbred designation tracking
- (b) **Platform Development** -- All software engineering, user interface design, database architecture, API development, and quality assurance, including migration from CPS's Existing Portal
- (c) **Hosting and Infrastructure** -- Cloud hosting, security, backups, monitoring, and system administration scaled for a network of 65,000+ breeders and 5,279 herdmarks
- (d) **Technical Support** -- Breeder-facing technical support for Platform usage, account management, herdmark inquiries, and troubleshooting
- (e) **Analytics and Data Products** -- Development of industry analytics, cross-breed benchmarking tools, genetic diversity metrics, and data-derived intelligence products
- (f) **Ongoing Enhancement** -- Continuous improvement of Platform features, performance, and capabilities based on breeder feedback, Breed Committee input, and industry trends
- (g) **Data Migration** -- Complete migration of all records from CPS's Existing Portal into BarnOS, including data validation, deduplication, integrity verification, and parallel operation during transition

**2.3 CPS Contributions.** CPS shall provide:

- (a) **Institutional Authority** -- CPS's official endorsement of the Platform as the authorized digital registration system for all CPS-registered breeds and crossbred designations
- (b) **Breeder Network Access** -- Introduction of the Platform to the Breeder Network through CPS's communication channels, events, publications, and direct outreach across all breed divisions
- (c) **Breed Standards Governance** -- Ongoing maintenance and communication of breed standards, registration rules, and classification criteria for all registered breeds, through CPS's Breed Committees, for integration into the Platform
- (d) **Herdmark System Access** -- Complete access to the Herdmark System, including all herdmark records, assignment rules, and governance procedures for integration into the Platform
- (e) **Historical Data Access** -- Complete access to Historical Records, including all data maintained in the Existing Portal, for migration into the Platform, subject to Section 5
- (f) **Show and Event Integration** -- Facilitation of Platform adoption at all CPS-sanctioned shows, sales, and events, including coordination with show committees and superintendents
- (g) **Industry Relationships** -- Introductions to allied industry organizations, university extension programs, genetics companies, and breed associations where mutually beneficial
- (h) **Subject Matter Expertise** -- Ongoing consultation on multi-breed swine genetics, breed improvement programs, crossbreeding strategies, and industry practices to inform Platform development

**2.4 Phase 1 -- Registry Modernization (Months 1-8).** During the Registry Modernization Phase, Sounder will develop and deploy:

- (a) **Multi-breed digital registration platform** -- Web-based system replacing the Existing Portal for all CPS registered breeds and crossbred designations, including litter notification, individual registration, transfer of ownership, and breed-specific registration workflows
- (b) **Breeder portal** -- Secure, authenticated portal enabling breeders to submit registrations across all breeds, view pedigrees, track performance data, manage herd records, maintain herdmark information, and communicate with CPS
- (c) **Herdmark management system** -- Digital herdmark assignment, transfer, renewal, and tracking system for all 5,279 active herdmarks, including historical herdmark records and succession tracking
- (d) **Show management platform** -- Comprehensive show management system for CPS-sanctioned events, including entry management, results recording, classification scoring, champion tracking, point accumulation, and show schedule coordination across 200+ annual events
- (e) **Pedigree database migration and verification** -- Complete migration of Historical Records from the Existing Portal and legacy systems into the Platform's structured database, including data validation, deduplication, cross-breed lineage verification, and integrity auditing across all breed registries
- (f) **Breeder CRM foundation** -- Customer relationship management system modeling each breeder as a structured entity with registration history across breeds, herd composition, herdmark assignments, performance records, show participation, and contact information
- (g) **Historical data import** -- Ingestion and normalization of records for approximately 65,412 breeders and 5,279 herdmarks across all breed registries, including pedigrees, performance records, registration histories, show records, and Existing Portal transaction data
- (h) **Multi-breed registration engine** -- Configurable registration workflow engine supporting breed-specific requirements, crossbred designation rules, and Breed Committee policies for each registered breed
- (i) **CPS staff training** -- Comprehensive training program for CPS staff on Platform administration, multi-breed registration workflows, herdmark management, show management, breeder support procedures, and reporting tools
- (j) **Parallel operation period** -- Minimum thirty (30) day period of parallel operation between the Existing Portal and the Platform to ensure data integrity and operational continuity before full cutover

**2.5 Phase 2 -- Platform Operations (Months 9+).** Following the Registry Modernization Phase, Sounder will expand to ongoing operations including:

- (a) **Registry platform hosting and maintenance** -- Continuous operation, monitoring, security patching, and performance optimization of the multi-breed digital registration system at scale
- (b) **Breeder CRM operations** -- Ongoing breeder relationship management, communication tools, segmentation analytics, and engagement tracking across all breed divisions
- (c) **Performance analytics dashboards** -- Interactive dashboards for breeders and CPS staff providing genetic trend analysis, breed improvement metrics, cross-breed performance comparisons, and herd benchmarking
- (d) **Cross-breed pedigree intelligence** -- Advanced pedigree analysis tools including inbreeding coefficient calculations, lineage visualization, mating recommendations, genetic diversity metrics, and cross-breed lineage tracing
- (e) **Show management operations** -- Ongoing digital management of 200+ CPS-sanctioned shows annually, including entry processing, results recording, point tracking, year-end awards calculation, and show committee coordination
- (f) **Herdmark management operations** -- Ongoing administration of the digital Herdmark System, including new assignments, transfers, renewals, suspensions, and succession management
- (g) **Breeder engagement analytics** -- Monitoring of breeder activity, registration volume trends across breeds, network growth, herdmark utilization, show participation patterns, and churn indicators to inform CPS outreach and retention strategies
- (h) **BarnOS Premium features** -- Advanced tools offered as optional subscription upgrades for breeders, including enhanced analytics, mating planners, marketing tools, multi-herd management, cross-breed analysis, and show history portfolios
- (i) **Data licensing products** -- Development and distribution of anonymized, aggregated industry analytics products derived from Platform data, for sale to genetics companies, feed manufacturers, equipment suppliers, pharmaceutical companies, and academic researchers
- (j) **Platform enhancement roadmap** -- Quarterly review and prioritization of new features, integrations, and improvements based on Breeder Network feedback, Breed Committee recommendations, and industry developments

## 3. FEES, REVENUE SHARING, AND PAYMENT

### Phase 1 -- Registry Modernization

**3.1 Platform Build Fee.** During the Registry Modernization Phase (months 1 through 8), CPS shall pay Sounder a one-time platform development fee in the range of **$35,000 to $50,000** (the "Build Fee"), with the exact amount to be determined upon completion of a detailed scope assessment, including an audit of the Existing Portal and data migration requirements, within thirty (30) days of the Effective Date and memorialized in a written amendment to this Agreement. The Build Fee reflects the substantial scope of multi-breed platform development, migration of 65,000+ breeder records, integration of 5,279 herdmarks, and replacement of the Existing Portal.

**3.2 Build Fee Payment Schedule.** The Build Fee shall be payable in four (4) milestone-based installments:

| Milestone | Payment | Timing |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| Project Kickoff & Data Migration Commencement | 30% of Build Fee | Upon Effective Date |
| Data Migration Complete & Registration Engine Beta | 30% of Build Fee | Upon verified migration of Historical Records and functional multi-breed registration beta (est. Month 3) |
| Show Management Platform & Breeder Portal Beta | 20% of Build Fee | Upon delivery of show management system and breeder portal beta (est. Month 5) |
| Platform Go-Live & Existing Portal Decommission | 20% of Build Fee | Upon production launch and successful parallel operation period (est. Month 8) |

### Phase 2 -- Platform Operations

**3.3 Monthly Base Fee.** Beginning in Month 9 and continuing throughout the term of this Agreement, CPS shall pay Sounder a monthly base fee in the range of **$8,000 to $15,000** (the "Base Fee"), with the exact amount to be determined based on the final scope of ongoing services, network scale, and operational complexity, and memorialized in a written amendment prior to the commencement of Phase 2.

### Revenue Sharing

**3.4 Per-Registration Fee.** For each animal registration processed through the Platform (including new registrations, litter notifications, and transfers of ownership across all breeds and crossbred designations), Sounder shall receive a technology fee of **$1.50 to $2.50 per registration** (the "Registration Technology Fee"). The per-unit rate reflects the high volume of registrations processed through CPS annually. The exact fee shall be established prior to Platform go-live and shall be collected by CPS as part of the standard registration fee and remitted to Sounder monthly. The Registration Technology Fee shall not increase the total registration fee charged to breeders without mutual written agreement.

**3.5 Breeder Subscription Revenue.** Sounder shall offer BarnOS Premium subscription features to members of the Breeder Network at a monthly subscription price of **$50 to $100 per breeder** (the "Premium Subscription"). Revenue from Premium Subscriptions shall be split as follows:

| Party | Share | Rationale |
|-------|-------|-----------|
| Sounder | 70% | Platform development, hosting, support |
| CPS | 30% | Breeder network access, institutional endorsement |

Given the scale of CPS's Breeder Network (65,412 breeders), even modest adoption rates represent substantial revenue. At 5% adoption (approximately 3,270 breeders), Premium Subscription revenue would range from $163,500 to $327,000 per month before split.

**3.6 Data Licensing Revenue.** Revenue generated from the sale of anonymized, aggregated industry analytics products derived from Platform data shall be split as follows:

| Party | Share | Rationale |
|-------|-------|-----------|
| Sounder | 60% | Analytics development, infrastructure, sales |
| CPS | 40% | Source data access, institutional credibility, industry relationships |

All data licensing products shall be subject to the data governance provisions of Section 5. No individually identifiable breeder data or proprietary breed standard information shall be included in data licensing products without CPS's prior written approval.

**3.7 Show Management Fees.** For each CPS-sanctioned show or exhibition managed through the Platform, Sounder shall receive a technology fee of **$500 to $1,000 per event** (the "Show Management Fee"), depending on show size, breed divisions, and complexity. CPS sanctions approximately 200+ shows annually. Show Management Fee revenue shall be split as follows:

| Party | Share | Rationale |
|-------|-------|-----------|
| Sounder | 50% | Platform tools, technical support, results processing |
| CPS | 50% | Show sanctioning, judge coordination, event authority |

**3.8 Herdmark Management Fee.** For each active herdmark maintained in the Platform, Sounder shall receive an annual technology fee of **$5.00 per herdmark per year** (the "Herdmark Management Fee"). With 5,279 active herdmarks, annual Herdmark Management Fee revenue is approximately $26,395. Herdmark Management Fee revenue shall be split as follows:

| Party | Share | Rationale |
|-------|-------|-----------|
| Sounder | 80% | Herdmark system digitization, maintenance, tracking |
| CPS | 20% | Herdmark governance, assignment authority |

**3.9 Revenue Reporting.** Sounder shall provide CPS with a monthly revenue report detailing all Revenue Streams, including: (a) total registrations processed by breed and Registration Technology Fees earned; (b) active Premium Subscriptions and associated revenue; (c) data licensing sales and associated revenue; (d) shows managed and Show Management Fees earned; and (e) active herdmarks and Herdmark Management Fees earned. Reports shall be delivered by the tenth (10th) business day of each month for the preceding month's activity.

**3.10 Revenue Share Payments.** CPS's share of Revenue Stream income shall be calculated monthly and paid within thirty (30) days of the revenue report described in Section 3.9. Sounder's share of revenues collected by CPS (Registration Technology Fees) shall be remitted to Sounder within thirty (30) days of collection.

**3.11 Audit Rights.** Each Party shall have the right, upon thirty (30) days' written notice and no more than once per calendar year, to audit the other Party's books and records related to Revenue Streams at the auditing Party's expense. If an audit reveals an underpayment of more than 5%, the underpaying Party shall bear the cost of the audit and remit the underpayment within thirty (30) days.

### General Payment Terms

**3.12 Payment Terms.** All invoices are due within thirty (30) days of the invoice date. Invoices for the Base Fee are issued on the first business day of each month. Late payments accrue interest at the lesser of 1.5% per month or the maximum rate permitted by applicable law.

**3.13 Taxes.** All fees and revenue share payments are exclusive of applicable taxes. Each Party is responsible for its own tax obligations arising from income received under this Agreement.

**3.14 Trogdon Farms.** For clarity, the Trogdon Farms Founding Partner Agreement referenced in Section 1.15 is a separate arrangement between Sounder and Trogdon Farms. Trogdon Farms' access to BarnOS does not affect CPS's obligations or revenue share under this Agreement. Registrations submitted by Trogdon Farms through the Platform are subject to the same Registration Technology Fee as all other breeders.

**4.1 Initial Term.** This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for an initial term of **forty-eight (48) months** (the "Initial Term"). The extended Initial Term reflects the scale of the data migration, the complexity of multi-breed platform development, and the magnitude of the Parties' mutual investment in this partnership.

**4.2 Renewal.** Following the Initial Term, this Agreement shall automatically renew for successive **twelve (12) month** periods (each a "Renewal Term") unless either Party provides written notice of non-renewal at least one hundred twenty (120) days prior to the end of the then-current term.

**4.3 Rate Adjustment.** Upon renewal, the Base Fee may be adjusted by mutual written agreement of the Parties. Revenue sharing percentages established in Sections 3.4 through 3.8 shall remain fixed for the duration of the Initial Term and may be renegotiated upon renewal with ninety (90) days' written notice by either Party. If the Parties cannot agree on adjusted revenue sharing terms within the notice period, the existing terms shall continue for the Renewal Term.

**4.4 Partnership Review.** The Parties shall conduct a formal partnership review no later than ninety (90) days prior to the expiration of the Initial Term to evaluate Platform performance, revenue outcomes, breeder adoption across breeds, show management effectiveness, herdmark system performance, strategic alignment, and terms for renewal.

## 5. DATA OWNERSHIP AND INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

**5.1 CPS Registry Data.** CPS retains full and exclusive ownership of all registry data, including: (a) Historical Records; (b) all pedigree records, registration records, and performance data created or maintained through the Platform across all breeds; (c) breeder account information and contact data; (d) herdmark records, assignment histories, and Herdmark System governance records; (e) show results, sanction records, and classification records; and (f) all data migrated from the Existing Portal. Sounder's access to CPS registry data is solely for the purpose of operating the Platform and performing services under this Agreement.

**5.2 Breed Standards, Registration Authority, and Herdmark Governance.** CPS retains exclusive ownership of all Breed Standards, registration rules, classification criteria, Breed Committee policies, herdmark assignment rules, show sanctioning criteria, and institutional policies. CPS's Registration Authority is not transferable and is not affected by this Agreement. All registration decisions (approvals, denials, revocations), herdmark assignments, and show sanctions remain the sole prerogative of CPS.

**5.3 BarnOS Platform.** BarnOS, including all platform architecture, source code, database schemas, user interface designs, analytics engines, API infrastructure, multi-breed registration engine logic, algorithm implementations, and software methodologies, is and remains the exclusive intellectual property of Sounder. Nothing in this Agreement transfers ownership of any Sounder IP to CPS.

**5.4 Joint Analytics.** Analytics products, reports, and intelligence outputs that integrate CPS registry data with Sounder's analytical infrastructure are jointly owned by the Parties ("Joint Analytics"). Neither Party may license, sell, or distribute Joint Analytics to third parties without the prior written consent of the other Party, except as expressly provided in Sections 3.6 and 3.7.

**5.5 Breeder Data.** Individual breeder information (herd records, herdmark details, personal contact information, financial transactions, private performance data) is considered CPS registry data subject to Section 5.1. Sounder shall handle all breeder personal information in compliance with applicable privacy laws and shall not share individually identifiable breeder data with third parties without CPS's express written authorization.

**5.6 Data Licensing Governance.** All data licensing products described in Section 3.6 shall be derived from anonymized, aggregated data only. Each data licensing product must be approved in writing by CPS prior to commercial distribution. CPS may withhold approval if the product could reasonably compromise breeder privacy, competitive positions of individual breeders, herdmark confidentiality, or CPS's institutional interests.

**5.7 Platform Improvements.** Any improvements, modifications, or derivative works to BarnOS developed during the performance of this Agreement, including multi-breed registration workflows, herdmark management tools, show management features, and cross-breed pedigree analytics methods, are the exclusive property of Sounder. Domain-specific configurations, breed standard rules, and registration logic developed in collaboration with CPS may be used by Sounder in other contexts, provided that CPS's proprietary Breed Standards, herdmark records, and registration criteria are not disclosed to competing registries.

**5.8 Data Portability.** Upon termination, CPS shall receive a complete export of all CPS registry data as described in Section 8.5. The Platform software, analytics infrastructure, and BarnOS source code are not included in the data export.

## 6. SERVICE LEVELS

**6.1 Availability.** Sounder shall maintain Platform availability of no less than **99.5%** measured monthly, excluding scheduled maintenance windows communicated at least 48 hours in advance. Scheduled maintenance shall be performed during off-peak hours (10 PM - 6 AM ET) whenever reasonably practicable. The 99.5% availability target reflects the mission-critical nature of the Platform for a network of 65,000+ breeders and 200+ annual shows.

**6.2 Registration Processing.** Digital registrations submitted through the Platform shall be queued for CPS review within one (1) business day of submission. System processing time (exclusive of CPS staff review) shall not exceed four (4) hours for standard registrations across all breeds.

**6.3 Herdmark Operations.** Herdmark assignment requests submitted through the Platform shall be queued for CPS review within one (1) business day of submission. Herdmark transfers and succession changes shall be processed within two (2) business days of CPS approval.

**6.4 Response Times.**

| Priority | Description | Response Time | Resolution Target |
|----------|-------------|---------------|-------------------|
| Critical | Platform outage; data integrity issue; registration system failure; herdmark system failure; show management failure during active show | 1 business hour | 4 business hours |
| High | Breeder portal inaccessible; analytics dashboard failure; show management system down during pre-show period; breed-specific registration workflow failure | 2 business hours | 12 business hours |
| Standard | Feature request; report inquiry; non-urgent bug; breeder support escalation; Breed Committee configuration request | 1 business day | 5 business days |

Standard business hours: 8 AM - 6 PM ET, Monday - Friday.

**6.5 Delivery Schedule.** Monthly revenue reports per Section 3.9 shall be delivered by the tenth (10th) business day of each month. Analytics dashboards shall be updated no less than weekly during the Operations Phase. Breed-specific performance reports shall be published within five (5) business days of data availability.

**6.6 Show Support.** For CPS-sanctioned shows managed through the Platform, Sounder shall provide dedicated technical support beginning two (2) business days prior to the show and continuing through one (1) business day following the show's conclusion. Response time during active shows shall not exceed thirty (30) minutes for Critical priority issues and one (1) hour for High priority issues. Given the volume of CPS-sanctioned shows (200+ annually), Sounder shall maintain a show support calendar coordinated with CPS no less than quarterly.

**6.7 Quarterly Review.** Sounder shall participate in quarterly partnership review meetings with CPS leadership to review Platform performance, service level compliance, revenue outcomes, Breeder Network adoption metrics across breeds, show management performance, herdmark system operations, and the product enhancement roadmap.

**6.8 Remedies.** If Sounder fails to meet the availability commitment in Section 6.1 for two (2) consecutive months, CPS may request a service credit equal to 10% of the applicable monthly Base Fee for each month of non-compliance, up to a maximum credit of one (1) month's Base Fee per calendar quarter.

## 7. EXCLUSIVITY

**7.1 Exclusive Technology Partner.** During the term of this Agreement, Sounder shall serve as the exclusive technology partner for CPS's digital registration, breeder portal, herdmark management, show management, and registry management systems. CPS shall not engage any third-party technology provider to develop or operate competing digital registration, herdmark management, or breeder management platforms during the term.

**7.2 Scope of Exclusivity.** Exclusivity applies to: (a) digital registration and pedigree management platforms for all CPS breeds and crossbred designations; (b) breeder-facing portal and CRM systems; (c) herdmark management and tracking systems; (d) show management technology for CPS-sanctioned events; and (e) performance analytics and genetic evaluation tools. Exclusivity does not restrict CPS from using third-party tools for general office administration, accounting, email, or other functions outside the scope of this Agreement.

**7.3 Sounder Non-Compete.** During the term of this Agreement, Sounder shall not develop, operate, or provide technology for a competing all-breed pedigree swine registry platform. This restriction acknowledges the unique and comprehensive nature of CPS's multi-breed registry and the strategic significance of this partnership. This restriction does not prevent Sounder from serving single-breed registries (such as the National Swine Registry), other segments of the swine industry (commercial operations, genetics companies, feed suppliers), or other livestock species.

**7.4 Strategic Significance.** The Parties acknowledge that this exclusivity arrangement reflects the strategic importance of the partnership: CPS's comprehensive multi-breed registry, combined with Sounder's BarnOS platform, creates a unified industry data layer spanning all major purebred and crossbred swine designations. The Parties shall work collaboratively to maximize the value of this position for breeders, the industry, and both organizations.

**7.5 Exclusivity Review.** Exclusivity terms shall be reviewed as part of the partnership review described in Section 4.4. If either Party believes the exclusivity arrangement is no longer serving the partnership's interests, the Parties shall negotiate in good faith to modify the terms.

## 8. TERMINATION

**8.1 Termination for Cause.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon ninety (90) days' written notice if the other Party materially breaches any provision of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within the notice period. The extended cure period reflects the complexity and scale of the partnership.

**8.2 Termination for Convenience.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon one hundred eighty (180) days' written notice. If CPS terminates for convenience during the Initial Term, CPS shall pay an early termination fee equal to six (6) months of the then-current Base Fee. If Sounder terminates for convenience during the Initial Term, Sounder shall provide transition support as described in Section 8.5 at no additional cost to CPS and shall continue operating the Platform for the full notice period.

**8.3 Termination for Non-Performance.** If Sounder fails to achieve Platform go-live within twelve (12) months of the Effective Date (four months beyond the planned Phase 1 completion), CPS may terminate this Agreement immediately with no early termination fee, and Sounder shall refund any Build Fee payments for undelivered milestones.

**8.4 Effect of Termination.** Upon termination: (a) each Party shall pay all fees and revenue shares accrued through the effective date of termination; (b) Sounder shall continue operating the Platform for a transition period of up to one hundred twenty (120) days at the then-current Base Fee; (c) each Party shall return or destroy the other Party's Confidential Information within thirty (30) days following the transition period.

**8.5 Data Export and Transition.** Within sixty (60) days of the termination effective date, Sounder shall make available to CPS a complete structured export of: (a) all CPS registry data, including pedigree records across all breeds, registration records, performance data, breeder account information, herdmark records, and show results, in industry-standard format (CSV, JSON, or XML); (b) all Joint Analytics reports and products produced during the term; (c) complete herdmark registry data in structured format with full assignment and transfer histories; and (d) documentation of all data schemas, field definitions, breed-specific configurations, and record relationships sufficient for CPS to migrate to a replacement system. The extended export period reflects the scale of CPS's data. BarnOS source code, platform architecture, and analytics engine are Sounder IP and are not included in the data export.

**8.6 Post-Termination Revenue.** Revenue share obligations for Revenue Streams generated prior to the termination effective date shall survive termination and be settled within sixty (60) days of termination. Premium Subscriptions active at the time of termination shall be honored through the end of their current billing period, with revenue shares paid accordingly.

**9.5 Public Announcements.** Neither Party shall issue press releases, marketing materials, or public statements regarding this partnership without the prior written approval of the other Party. The Parties shall collaborate on a joint announcement of the partnership at a mutually agreed time.

**9.6 Duration.** Confidentiality obligations survive termination of this Agreement for a period of five (5) years.

## 10. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

**10.1 Cap.** Each Party's total aggregate liability under this Agreement shall not exceed the greater of: (a) the total fees and revenue share payments received by such Party during the twelve (12) months preceding the event giving rise to the claim; or (b) $250,000. The increased liability cap reflects the scale and strategic significance of this partnership.

2. Herdmark records shall be verified against CPS's official herdmark registry with 100% accuracy.
3. Pedigree lineage chains shall be verified to a minimum depth of five (5) generations where records exist.
4. Duplicate breeder records shall be identified, flagged, and resolved in consultation with CPS staff.
5. CPS shall designate a data migration liaison to participate in weekly migration review meetings.
6. A complete migration audit report shall be delivered to CPS upon completion of the migration, documenting record counts, validation results, exceptions, and resolution status.
