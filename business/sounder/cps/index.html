History & Evolution
The all-breed registry that grew to dominate American swine registration — 17× larger than NSR
Early 1900s
Individual breed associations — Hampshire, Duroc, Yorkshire, Berkshire, Poland China, and others — each maintained separate registries. Breeders with multiple breeds needed accounts at every association.
Mid-Century
The show pig market explodes. County and state fairs become the primary marketplace for purebred swine. Breeders need a single registration system that handles all breeds in one place.
CPS Founded
Certified Pedigree Swine established as the all-breed alternative. One membership, one herdmark, registrations for Hampshire, Duroc, Berkshire, Poland China, Spot, Yorkshire, Landrace, Chester White, Hereford, Tamworth — and crossbred designations.
Growth Era
CPS leverages the show pig circuit to acquire members at scale. Every county fair, state fair, and national show drives breeder registration. The herdmark system — 2–4 character codes — becomes the universal identifier for breeders.
2010s
CPS launches a basic online portal for registrations and breeder directory. Still limited in functionality, but more advanced than NSR's paper-only system. Breeder count passes 60,000.
2026
With 65,412 breeders and 5,279 registered herdmarks, CPS is the definitive swine registry. Sounder partnership proposed — transform the basic portal into a full industry platform. 65K breeders, one graph.
Current Operations

Certified Pedigree Swine processes registrations for every recognized swine breed under a single system. A breeder with Hampshire, Duroc, Spot, and Poland China animals registers all of them through CPS with one herdmark — no separate breed association accounts needed.

The herdmark system assigns each breeder a unique 2–4 character code (e.g., TRG, FFM, HPG). This identifier appears on every registration certificate and tattoo, creating a universal namespace for the purebred swine industry. CPS maintains 5,279 active herdmarks.

Geographic concentration is heavily Midwest and South: Indiana leads with 14,552 breeders (22%), followed by Oklahoma (9,177), Texas (9,174), Illinois (5,542), and Ohio (4,047). These five states account for nearly two-thirds of all CPS members.

CPS dominates the show pig world. The majority of show pig registrations flow through CPS, making it the gatekeeper for county fairs, state fairs, and national shows across the country.

The CPS breeder directory is the most comprehensive in the US, but the online portal remains basic — functional for simple registrations but lacking CRM, analytics, real-time processing, mobile optimization, and marketplace capabilities. Many breeders dual-register with both CPS and NSR.
