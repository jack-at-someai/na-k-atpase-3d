Company Profile

From magazine to auction empire

1990
Roland Schumaker founds The Show Circuit Magazine — a livestock publication covering the show ring across species.
2015
SC Online Sales launched by Dave Guyer and Roland Schumaker. Multi-species online livestock auction platform built in Robinson, Illinois.
2017
10,000+ registered users and 220,000 bids placed. Platform gains traction across cattle and sheep communities.
2018
Q1 page views reach 9.3 million — 166% year-over-year growth. The media flywheel is working.

COVID pivot — virtual show platform for cancelled 4-H/FFA county fairs. Industry shifts online permanently.
2024
Swine division formalized. Sheep and goat division in rapid growth. Three independent species divisions operating under one roof.
2026

Industry

The show stock ecosystem

The show stock market spans cattle, sheep, goats, and pigs with distinct buyer communities but overlapping breeders. COVID permanently shifted the industry to online — many operations never returned to in-person-only. No platform has unified cross-species intelligence. Sellers operating in multiple species get no combined analytics.

Digital agriculture marketplace projected to grow from $16.45B (2025) to $43.73B (2033) at 13% CAGR. The infrastructure layer connecting breeders, buyers, registries, and show results across all species does not exist yet. Every platform operates in its own silo.

The Show Circuit Magazine's 50K+ audience is an untapped data asset. Readership patterns predict auction behavior. Magazine subscribers who engage with cattle content buy at cattle sales. The attribution link between media consumption and bidding behavior has never been measured — because nobody has built the graph to connect them. Charlotte builds that graph.
