Charlotte Substrate

How Charlotte OS models the instrument fund — five primitives encoding every entity, relationship, measurement, event, and rule in the PPP portfolio.

NODE
Each instrument (maker, year, provenance chain), each person (dealer, owner, player, certifier). The Amati 1657 is a node. Paul Prier is a node. The provenance chain linking them is a sequence of edges between nodes.
EDGE
Purchase, sale, certification, appraisal, consignment, ownership transfer. Every transaction and attestation is a typed, timestamped edge connecting two nodes in the graph.
METRIC
Purchase price, retail value, discount percentage, appreciation rate, days held, annual return. Continuous measurements attached to nodes and edges that drive portfolio analytics.
SIGNAL
Acquisition, appraisal update, sale, authentication, market discovery. Discrete events emitted by nodes that trigger downstream computation and portfolio rebalancing.
PROTOCOL
Buy-below-retail threshold (15–35%), hold-for-appreciation rules, certification requirements, fund allocation limits. The governance layer that constrains how the fund operates.
