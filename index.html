Abstract

These projects exist to calibrate the best substrate to facilitate an artificially intelligent operating system. Each module ‚Äî whether a game, simulation, visualization, or tool ‚Äî stress-tests a different facet of the problem space: real-time rendering, graph traversal, state management, algorithmic complexity, human interaction patterns, and domain modeling. They are not portfolio pieces. They are research instruments.

The ultimate target is Charlotte ‚Äî a graph-native knowledge graph infrastructure where everything is encoded as five primitives (Node, Metric, Signal, Edge, Protocol) and time is the primary axis of truth. Charlotte observes reality as it unfolds: identities emit signals on measurable dimensions, relationships form edges, and protocols forecast what should happen next. No schemas, no migrations, no architectural rewrites between domains.

The work here accelerates the research and implementation leading to Draft 1 of Charlotte's knowledge graph infrastructure ‚Äî and ultimately our first production version that we plan to iterate into a competitor to Neo4j, Amazon Neptune, Power BI, and the rest of the fragmented data tooling landscape. The thesis is simple: nobody offers the full pipeline end-to-end. Charlotte will be the first system that handles data ingestion to tooling deployment in real time, for any client, in any domain, without rewiring the architecture. One substrate. Every vertical. Observable reality as a service.

The Research Lab

Every project on this page is a research instrument — not a portfolio piece. Each one stress-tests a facet of Charlotte's substrate: graph traversal, temporal modeling, spatial perception, swarm intelligence, domain modeling, signal processing. The lab has produced 12 published research papers across venues including Nature, IEEE, ACM, NeurIPS, and Cognitive Science. Charlotte is validated across four domains: human behavior (LineLeap), biological systems (Sounder/Trogdon showpigs), industrial equipment (ISG), and cultural artifacts (Prier Violins). The production dataset contains 27,200 nodes and 46,100 edges in a unified registry.

The Smart Village

The endgame is a purpose-built 50–200 acre property — a modern Main Street where 20–40 businesses all run Charlotte as their sole operating system. Coffee shops, restaurants, salons, veterinary clinics, co-working spaces — every storefront shares one PoS, one inventory system, one intelligence layer. No business in the cluster runs separate software. Ingestion of a new operation is a single scan and mirror — hours to days for an organized business. The property is simultaneously a revenue-generating commercial district, a PE demonstration environment, and a blueprint for civilization-scale infrastructure planning.

The Cluster Model

Each village is a self-sustaining operating system cluster that can interact with other clusters or operate in complete isolation. Sovereignty is architectural, not political. The network effect is superlinear: every business added makes every other business in the cluster smarter. Cross-cluster intelligence — foot traffic, supply chain, demand forecasting, labor optimization — compounds as clusters scale nationally. Imagine streamlining all semen delivery in the Midwest for every pig breeder on one Charlotte-powered network. That is one vertical, one region, $20M/year. Now multiply across every vertical Charlotte has already proven it can model.

The PE Thesis

Charlotte eliminates $770K–$3.4M/year in integration tax per portfolio company by replacing 5–8 disconnected tools with one substrate. Post-acquisition integration drops from 12–24 months to hours. Cross-portfolio intelligence is native, not engineered. Customer acquisition cost is near-zero — every deployment is a warm introduction through existing PE relationships, not cold outreach. LTV:CAC exceeds 50:1. Annual churn is <5% because abandoning Charlotte means abandoning your entire signal history — append-only, immutable, irreproducible.
